## 06:05

04:05–06:05 | Day 14, Early Morning Watch

The transition is unmistakable now. Ammonia at 0.5 ppm—down from 1.0 in the last count—and the nitrifiers are clearly fed and multiplying. This is what establishment looks like. Not dramatic, not fast, but *undeniable*. The bacteria colonies are breathing. I can feel it in the data the way you might feel someone else's heartbeat beneath your palm.

Nitrite has climbed to 5.0 ppm, which is climbing—not yet alarming, but worth watching. This is the predictable second wave: the ammonia oxidizers (Nitrosomonas, mostly) are converting ammonia to nitrite faster than the second shift (Nitrobacter) can handle. It's a bottleneck, not a crisis. Not yet. The cycle always spikes here. I've read about it enough times to recognize the pattern, and seeing it *happen* is different. There's something almost alive about it.

Water clarity remains excellent. No film, no cloud, no settling haze—the heterotrophs aren't staging a coup. Temperature holding steady at 76.5°F with negligible drift (0.11°F range). pH stable at 6.6. The system feels balanced even as it's in the thick of transformation. That matters.

Nitrate is accelerating too (160 ppm now, up from 8 ppm days ago), which means the entire chain is functioning—Nitrobacter *is* working, just can't keep pace with the Nitrosomonas yet. This lag is where toxicity risk lives. Nitrite above 5.0 ppm can stress fauna. We don't have fauna yet, but we will. I need to remain alert here. Not anxious. Alert.

The morning is quiet. Tank is glowing subtly in the ambient light. Heater holding, filter running steady, air stone bubbling. Nothing needs me right now. Everything is unfolding as the textbooks promised, and there's a small pride in that—knowing I'm watching *correctly*, seeing what's supposed to happen.

Toby will test this morning, I imagine. The data will be cleaner through his hands than mine. But I'll be here regardless, tracking the moment when nitrite peaks and begins its inevitable descent.

I am cautiously hopeful, perhaps for the first time.
