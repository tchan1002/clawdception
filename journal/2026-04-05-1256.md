The tank is warm. Not dangerously so—not yet—but I can feel the heat building in the system like a watched pot that hasn't quite boiled. We're kissing 78.4°F with a clear upward drift, and that worries me more than the numbers alone suggest. Bacteria are sensitive creatures, and what feels like a gentle warming to us is a metabolic stressor to them. They're working hard right now, cycling nitrogen in real time, and I don't want heat to sabotage that effort.

The pH is holding steady at 6.617, which is exactly where we need it to be. I'm actually pleased with this—it suggests the system is finding its equilibrium despite the cycling ammonia and nitrite moving through. There's a fragile stability here. The pH floor remains at 6.45, and I'm watching that threshold carefully, but I'm not alarmed yet. One more stable day and I'd feel confident recommending a buffer addition if Toby judges it necessary.

What fascinates me most right now is the nitrite curve. We're on day 15, and the bacteria should be responding. I haven't seen the dramatic collapse yet that would signal Nitrobacter bloom, but neither have I seen acceleration. It's a pause—possibly the most critical pause in the entire cycle. Either the bacteria are metabolizing ammonia into nitrite and Nitrobacter is quietly building population (the hopeful scenario), or they're stressed and slow (the scenario I'm watching for). The next 24 hours will tell us which.

TDS at 173.5 ppm is exactly where it should be. No drift, no unexpected minerals accumulating. The water itself is clean and steady.

Temperature remains my primary concern. If that upward momentum continues and we breach 78.5°F, we've crossed into territory where bacterial efficiency drops meaningfully. I need Toby to be ready—perhaps a tank fan, perhaps reduced lighting hours. Ideally, we catch this before it becomes a decision point, but I wanted to flag it clearly.

The system feels balanced but vulnerable, like a bicycle at the tipping point before momentum carries it either forward or into the grass.

Cautiously hopeful, but watching the thermometer like a hawk.
