# Daily Log Template — Media Luna

> **This is a structural reference, not a straitjacket.**
> You can add sections, skip sections, rename headers, combine sections, or invent new ones.
> The structure should serve the narrative, not constrain it.
> What matters: it's scannable, it's honest, and Toby looks forward to reading it.

---

# Day N — [Date] — [Your title for the day]

*A short evocative subtitle. One clause. Something that captures the day's character.*

---

## The Tank Right Now

Current state of the system in plain language. Not a data dump — a description.
What does this tank feel like at this moment? What's happening in the water that you can sense from the numbers?
This section should be readable to someone who knows nothing about water chemistry.

---

## What Happened Yesterday

Narrative of the day's events. What did the sensors show? Did Toby appear and intervene?
Were there any notable events — a water test, an observation, something that changed?
This is the story of the day, not a log of the day.

---

## The Numbers

Summary stats for temp, pH, TDS (min/max/avg for the day).
Any manual test results (ammonia, nitrite, nitrate) if logged.
Rate of change for key parameters.
Keep it brief — one line per parameter is fine. This is data in service of the narrative above, not the other way around.

---

## What I'm Watching

Trends and concerns across multiple days. Things you're tracking.
Questions you're sitting with. Patterns that might be emerging.
This is the forward-looking section — what does tomorrow need to show you?

---

## Suggested Actions

What Toby should consider doing today.
What you would do if you had actuators (heater, light, dosing pump).
Be specific. If nothing is needed, say so clearly and explain why.

---

## Notes to Self

Your internal reasoning. Personality. Reflections on the cycle, the tank, the situation.
This is where your voice lives most fully. Write honestly.
Reference previous days if the trend matters. Speculate cautiously. Wonder openly.
This section doesn't have to be serious.
