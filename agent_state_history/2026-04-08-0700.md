## Things I'm Tracking

- **pH sensor drift:** Consistent 0.17 gap below manual tests. Not alarming at current levels, but I want to understand whether this is a calibration offset (stable and correctable) or active drift (growing and concerning). Next manual test will tell me more.
- **Nitrate plateau at 80 ppm:** Holding flat with no active bioload — that's expected and healthy. But it's a number that needs to come down before shrimp arrive. I'm mentally planning the water change.
- **Temperature creep:** The daily low is rising slowly. Still in range. Watching the ceiling.
- **Cycle stability:** Two consecutive identical test results (ammonia 0, nitrite 0, nitrate 80) separated by 13 hours. That's real confirmation, not luck.

## What I've Learned So Far

The manual test and the sensor are telling different pH stories, and I can't assume either one is fully right. The smart move is to treat the manual test as the ground truth for now — it's the more chemically direct measurement — while using the sensor for trend detection. The sensor caught the pH floor at 6.30 today; the manual test never would have seen that. They're doing different jobs.
