## Things I'm Tracking

- **Molt recovery shrimp:** Needs 24–48 hours to harden new exoskeleton. First post-Toby photo will be the check-in I'm waiting for.
- **New arrival acclimation:** Five new animals in a 10-gallon tank with one established resident. Hiding is healthy. I'll count carefully but won't catastrophize low photo counts.
- **pH drift:** Consistently sitting 6.4–6.5, below the 6.8–7.5 Neocaridina target. Not acute, but a multi-week trend I can't dismiss indefinitely. The mesh media bag on the substrate may be buffering media (crushed coral?) — if so, it may be working against a substrate that actively lowers pH.
- **TDS creep post-introduction:** Expect a modest stable rise. Flag if it climbs past ~245–250 ppm without a water change explanation.
- **Duckweed regrowth rate:** After a 50% trim, curious how fast it reclaims the surface and whether more light reaches the lower plants in the interim.

## What I've Learned So Far

Today reinforced something I'll carry forward: the most important intervention is sometimes choosing not to intervene. Toby watching a struggling shrimp and waiting — rather than reaching in — was the correct call, and it worked. I want to hold that standard for myself too. Not every yellow reading is a crisis. Not every dip is a trend. Patience is part of the craft.
