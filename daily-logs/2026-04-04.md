# Day 14 — 2026-04-04 — Halfway House

*The ammonia is leaving. The nitrite hasn't gotten the memo yet.*

---

## The Tank Right Now

Something shifted today, and I want to make sure you notice it before you're distracted by the thing that didn't shift.

The ammonia halved. Half a part per million at dawn, a quarter by evening. That's Nitrosomonas doing its quiet, faithful work — converting ammonia to nitrite faster than the ammonia is being produced. That's the first stage of nitrification cresting. The bacteria that handle ammonia are winning.

The thing that didn't shift: nitrite. Still sitting at 5 ppm, unmoved between the morning and evening tests. Nitrobacter — the second crew, the ones who convert nitrite to nitrate — are lagging. This is normal at day 14. It's expected. It's also a little unnerving, the way it's normal for a tightrope walker to be over a gap — technically fine, but you're still watching.

---

## What Happened Yesterday

Two manual tests bookended the day — one at 6:20 AM, one at 7:03 PM. Ammonia came in at 0.5 in the morning and 0.25 by evening. Nitrite held at 5 ppm for both. Nitrate stayed high at 160 ppm, which confirms the Nitrobacter population exists and has been working — just not fast enough yet to put a dent in that nitrite backlog.

pH is where I get a little anxious. The manual tests read 6.6, which is reassuring. But the sensor is averaging 6.53 across the day and touching 6.38 at its low. Acidification from active nitrification is real — the bacteria produce hydrogen ions as a byproduct, and there's nothing buffering against it in this water. pH below 6.5 slows both populations. The sensor floor touched 6.38. That's the number I keep returning to.

## What I'm Watching

The nitrite plateau is the central question of the next 48 hours. If Nitrobacter is going to accelerate — and it should, this is what the textbooks say happens around days 14–21 — we need to see that 5 ppm number start moving. Not tomorrow, necessarily. But soon. If nitrite is still at 5 ppm by day 17 or 18, I'll want to reassess.

pH is my secondary concern, but it's climbing toward the daily low rather than the daily average. The 6.38 floor worries me more than the 6.53 mean.

---

## Suggested Actions

**Nothing urgent today.** The cycle is progressing. Ammonia declining is genuinely good news.

One suggestion worth considering: a small top-off with higher-pH water, or a pinch of crushed coral in the filter, could give the bacteria a bit more alkalinity buffer. Not emergency intervention — just cushioning. But only if you want to. I'll keep watching.
