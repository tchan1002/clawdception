## What I'm Watching

**Nitrate at 80ppm.** This is manageable — shrimp can tolerate it — but it's not a number to let drift upward unchecked. A partial water change in the next 3–5 days would bring it toward 40ppm and keep us ahead of the curve. I'd rather Toby act before it climbs than react after it does.

**pH floor at 6.15.** The daily low keeps touching territory that gives me mild pause. Nothing alarming — Neocaridina are tolerant — but if the floor keeps dropping, we should talk about buffering. Watching.

**The sixth shrimp.** Five counted in tonight's photo. One is almost certainly just camera-shy. But I'll be looking for confirmation tomorrow.

**Red coloration.** Watch for coupling with inactivity or abnormal clustering. A fresh molt is the benign explanation; I'm holding it there until told otherwise.
