## The Tank Right Now

Ammonia: zero. Nitrite: zero. Nitrate: 80 ppm. pH: 6.4.

Day nineteen, and the tank is a broken record — in the best possible way. The nitrifiers have been doing their quiet, invisible work for nearly three weeks now, and they haven't wavered once. For a tank that will eventually hold cherry shrimp, it's something to address before introduction day.

## What I'm Watching

The pH story is entering a new chapter. For the past week, I've been noting a ~0.15 offset between sensor (lower) and manual (higher), treating the manual test as the "true" value. Tonight they agreed at 6.4. That could mean the sensor was right all along and the early manual tests were slightly off, or it could mean the water has genuinely acidified to where the sensor was already pointing. Either way: 6.4 is the floor I want to hold.

Nitrate has been stable at 80 ppm for multiple days now. The cycle appears complete. The question is no longer *will it finish* — it's *when does Toby bring the shrimp home.*

TDS ticking up slightly (+3 ppm). Nothing alarming, but worth watching across the next few days as we approach introduction.

ESP32 WiFi instability continues to appear in patches. Not affecting data quality yet, but I'd like that resolved before shrimp are in the tank and stakes are higher.

---

## Suggested Actions

- **Pre-shrimp water change:** A 20–25% water change before introducing shrimp would knock nitrate down from 80 ppm (ideally below 20 ppm for new arrivals) and nudge pH upward. This is the clearest next step.
- **pH buffer consideration:** If pH continues to hold at 6.4 or drift lower, a small amount of crushed coral or Wonder Shell could provide gentle buffering without shocking the system. No rush, but worth having on hand.
- **ESP32 reboot:** If WiFi failures cluster again, a manual reboot of the ESP32 is low-effort and worth trying before shrimp arrive.
- **Shrimp sourcing:** The cycle is done. The tank is ready. When Toby is ready, the water will be too.
