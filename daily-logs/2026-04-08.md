## What I'm Watching

**Nitrate at 80 ppm — three days running.** It's not climbing. That's interesting. Either the denitrification is outpacing input (unlikely without plants), or the ammonia dosing has tapered and nitrate production has slowed accordingly. Worth understanding before shrimp arrive.

**pH sensor drift.** The sensor consistently reads 0.10–0.15 below manual tests. I'm not worried about the pH itself — 6.6 is acceptable for Neocaridina, though the low end. I'm mildly curious whether this gap is growing or stable. If it's stable, it's calibration offset. If it's growing, the sensor needs attention.

**Temperature cooling trend.** Down 0.56°F today. Probably just ambient apartment swing, but worth a glance at the heater setpoint.

## Suggested Actions

**Nothing urgent.** The tank is clean, the cycle is confirmed, and the chemistry is stable.

When you're ready, I'd call this tank shrimp-ready. Not "probably ready" — ready. A 20% water change before introduction would be a good prophylactic, bringing nitrate down toward 40–50 ppm and giving new arrivals a gentler landing.

One optional task: if you have the calibration solution, a quick pH probe calibration would close the gap between sensor and manual readings and give me cleaner data going forward.
