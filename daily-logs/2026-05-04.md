## The Tank Right Now

There's a shrimp on the mesh bag in the back corner — translucent, reddish, juvenilish — and behind it a whole ecosystem of infrastructure: dual sponge filters, ceramic media, a pump, a teal thermometer. Surface plants have gone full canopy overhead. The water is clear. This tank looks, in a word, *inhabited*. Not just running — actually lived in.

---

## What Happened

Today's drama was a false alarm wearing a convincing costume. The pH numbers went strange — dipping to 5.88, then swinging back — and temperature spiked to 77°F before settling. Alarming on paper. In reality: the temperature probe had slipped out of the water, and spent about forty minutes air-sampling instead of tank-sampling. Every downstream reading that depended on it was noise. Toby caught it, logged it at 22:33, and reseated the probe. The numbers calmed down immediately.

And then he fed the colony. Pellets, 22:34. One minute after the fix — efficient. The feeding gap had stretched past 78 hours, and that gets addressed now.

This morning there was also a logged maintenance event, which explains the gentle downward drift in TDS and pH from the daytime readings. Post-maintenance settling, nothing more.
