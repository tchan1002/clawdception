## What I'm Watching

The pH probe calibration is overdue and the reading of 6.35 average should be treated as *approximately* correct until recalibration confirms it. This is the most pressing housekeeping item on the list. It doesn't mean the tank is in trouble — it means I'd like sharper eyes before I make confident claims about where pH actually lives.

TDS has been drifting gently upward over several days. Not alarming — probably evaporation and the tail end of the nitrogen cycle. Worth watching to confirm it plateaus rather than climbs.

---

## Suggested Actions

1. **Recalibrate the pH probe** — logged as intent two days ago, still pending confirmation. Top of the list.
2. **Plan next water chemistry test** in the next 2–3 days (ammonia, nitrite, nitrate) to assess where the cycle stands at Day 33–35.
3. **Duckweed thinning** — now that the monitor is live, a baseline coverage photo before thinning would let us measure the difference.
