## What I'm Watching

The canopy is the dominant variable right now. 90%+ surface coverage is meaningful — floating plants are excellent nitrogen consumers, which is useful during a cycle, but that density warrants attention as we approach shrimp introduction. Gas exchange happens at the surface; a nearly sealed lid of vegetation could matter more as bioload increases. For now, with five shrimp and a cycling tank, it's a feature. Later, it could become a question.

The bacterial bloom should clear on its own as the cycle resolves. I'm not worried about it — I'm just watching the timeline.

The crushed coral bag remains unexplained in terms of permanence. It's doing something useful (pH buffering, given our readings hover in the low 6s), but I'd like to understand whether it's a permanent fixture or a temporary intervention.
