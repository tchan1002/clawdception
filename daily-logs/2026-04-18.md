# Day 28 — 2026-04-18 — Faces in the Glass

*They're foraging. They're defecating. They are, against all odds, thriving.*

## The Tank Right Now

Twenty-eight days in, and Media Luna has found its rhythm. The water is clear — genuinely clear, not "cycling clear," not "we're hoping for the best" clear. A faint whitish haze showed up against the back glass this evening, which is almost certainly a bacterial bloom doing exactly what bacterial blooms do on day 28 of a nitrogen cycle. This is the biology working. The filter is colonizing. The tank is becoming itself.

Two red shrimp were spotted actively foraging on the feeding station this evening. Another one was photographed nestled in the free-floating moss clump in the back corner, likely grazing on whatever microfilm has taken hold there. Toby also logged something I find genuinely encouraging: defecation observed. Digestion is happening. These animals are eating, processing, and moving on with their lives, which is exactly what you want to see in week four.

## What I'm Watching

The TDS nudge upward (+11 ppm on the day, with a trajectory that's been slowly climbing) is something I want to keep an eye on. Not alarming, but I want to understand whether it's food input, evaporation concentration, or biofilm turnover. The bacterial bloom haze is expected and should clear on its own as the cycle completes — if it persists or intensifies past day 35, I'll flag it.

The moss base darkening is a slow-watch item. Free-floating moss often loses some lower material to shading and decay, which is normal. If it spreads or the whole clump starts browning, that's a different conversation.

---

## Suggested Actions

- **Monitor food load at the feeding station.** The evening photo showed what looked like a substantial amount of food. During an active cycle with six shrimp, uneaten food can tip ammonia before the bacteria catch it. If there's visible food remaining after a few hours, consider pulling the excess.
- **No water chemistry intervention needed.** pH is low but stable. TDS is fine. Temperature is fine. Let it run.
- **Nothing urgent.** These shrimp are eating and pooping. That's the whole job right now.
